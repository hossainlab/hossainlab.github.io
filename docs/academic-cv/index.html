Research Statement

I am a computational biologist with a track record of building and deploying reproducible multi-omics frameworks to decode the molecular architecture of cancer and neurological diseases. My research addresses three interconnected questions: (1) How can integrative analysis of bulk RNA-seq, single-cell RNA-seq, and spatial transcriptomics identify robust immunological biomarkers and therapeutic targets within tumor microenvironments? (2) Can interpretable machine learning models trained on pan-cancer transcriptomic data reliably stratify patient prognosis and predict immunotherapy response? (3) What computational strategies best characterize dynamic cell-state transitions — such as tumor-associated macrophage reprogramming and microglial activation in neurodegeneration? My published work includes first- and corresponding-author pan-cancer analyses in Scientific Reports and BMC Genomics, six GitHub-hosted reproducible pipelines built on Nextflow, Docker, and nf-core standards, and GBD consortium contributions published in The Lancet family. My long-term goal is to develop scalable, interpretable computational methods for cell-state inference and immune landscape modeling, translating multi-omics discoveries into clinically actionable biomarkers and therapeutic hypotheses.
